## How to use this worksheet

Review in order and write only the decision-relevant note at each checkpoint. Start with the story pass, then the hero and cast, then chapter backgrounds and encounter sprites, and finish with palette and system consistency. Mark anything that needs a change with a clear action verb: rewrite, remove, add, reposition, recolor, rename, or verify.

### Fast review route

- [ ] Story pass — read the arc title, tagline, chapter titles, captions, and scene copy without looking at art.
- [ ] Art pass — inspect the hero, supporting characters, opponents, bosses, finishers, and backgrounds at actual display size.
- [ ] Consistency pass — compare palette, silhouette language, contrast, naming, and emotional escalation across chapters.
- [ ] Action pass — consolidate duplicate notes, rank fixes by impact, and record any final questions for implementation.

### Chapter 1 — Roots in the Square

Mara follows a living rhythm through the damage left by a rainless storm.

**Scene beat:** Mara braces the cracked roofs, then maps the root's pulse from beam to beam across the village. Its movement is not an attack but a trapped creature searching for the path it once knew. Fear and dry thunder shape the root tip into the Rootquake Hart, which bucks beneath the square whenever the villagers pull against it. Mara must ease each brace in sequence and guide the frightened manifestation toward open ground.

**Intent cue:** Settle the root

**Boss / escalation:** Rootquake Hart

### Chapter 5 — The Stone Barrier

Iven learns that saving his aqueduct requires opening the monument he built to endure.

**Scene beat:** The waking forest reaches the aqueduct and stops. Heat trapped in the foundation rises as the Kilnstone Titan, a volcanic guardian formed from Iven's certainty that strong work must never change. He and Mara must cool, brace, and reshape the structure without pretending it can remain untouched.

Commissioner Senn takes the last rain tree toward the capital in the name of survival.

### Chapter 7 — A Living Channel

The valley rebuilds its waterworks as a corridor shared with the migrating forest.

**Scene beat:** As the wider watershed fails, Mara asks the villages to stop defending an aqueduct that can only preserve one shrinking season. Together they reshape terraces, gates, and bridges into a route where water and roots can move side by side. Discarded masonry and cut roots bind into the Oldcourse Colossus, a rootbound monument to every shortcut the valley took around living change. The community dismantles it piece by piece, reusing each stone in the new channel.

**Intent cue:** Build the living channel

### Chapter 8 — When the Trees Return

Song, engineering, and community must move the forest before its final storm fades.

**Scene beat:** The corridor opens, but the exhausted trees no longer remember how to begin their migration. Mara tunes the structures, Iven releases the gates, and Orra invites the whole valley to carry the song she once guarded alone. The forest's unspent weather blooms overhead as the Rainstar Canopy, a cosmic tangle of lightning and luminous seed paths. By opening every channel in sequence, the valley turns that dangerous pressure into rain and gives the trees a road home.

**Intent cue:** Sing the forest home

Compare the theme colors against the chapter accent pairs above. Check readability in both quiet story scenes and high-intensity combat moments; the palette should support hierarchy, not just atmosphere.

- [ ] Palette — colors are intentional, distinguishable, accessible, and consistent with the story’s emotional arc.
- [ ] Backgrounds — focal areas remain clear behind characters and copy; no important detail is lost to crop or contrast.
- [ ] Sprites — silhouettes, edge treatment, animation frames, and scale feel like one production family.
- [ ] Naming — titles, captions, IDs, filenames, and visible labels agree.
- [ ] Escalation — chapter art and boss spectacle build toward the finale rather than peaking too early.
- [ ] Polish — no accidental artifacts, broken alpha, awkward tangents, duplicated motifs, or unfinished frames.

**Write specific corrections, not just preferences. Name the asset and the proposed direction when possible.**
