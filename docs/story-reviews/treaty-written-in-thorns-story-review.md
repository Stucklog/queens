## How to use this worksheet

Review in order and write only the decision-relevant note at each checkpoint. Start with the story pass, then the hero and cast, then chapter backgrounds and encounter sprites, and finish with palette and system consistency. Mark anything that needs a change with a clear action verb: rewrite, remove, add, reposition, recolor, rename, or verify.

### Fast review route

- [ ] Story pass — read the arc title, tagline, chapter titles, captions, and scene copy without looking at art.
- [ ] Art pass — inspect the hero, supporting characters, opponents, bosses, finishers, and backgrounds at actual display size.
- [ ] Consistency pass — compare palette, silhouette language, contrast, naming, and emotional escalation across chapters.
- [ ] Action pass — consolidate duplicate notes, rank fixes by impact, and record any final questions for implementation.

### Chapter 1 — The Living Signature

Sign a peace whose promises can answer back.

**Scene beat:** Before both courts, Liora and Ren arrange the treaty's living clauses so neither kingdom can hide an army inside a careful definition. They sign before either understands that the vine will punish private lies as fiercely as public ones. The discarded loopholes twist together as The Covenant Briar, defending every ambiguity the courts hoped to exploit. Liora and Ren can cut through it only by stating what each side is truly afraid to lose.

**Intent cue:** Sign the living treaty

**Boss / escalation:** The Covenant Briar

### Chapter 2 — Truth Under Skin

Learn the cost of courtesy without honesty.

**Scene beat:** Their first private negotiation becomes a lesson in strategic honesty: omissions sting, polished evasions draw blood, and truths offered as weapons grow thorns too. Liora admits she expects Ren to betray her, while Ren admits he came prepared to deserve that fear. Their rehearsed courtly answers take form as The Courtesy Shade, speaking each pleasing falsehood in a beloved voice. Naming the motives beneath those answers clears their skin and establishes the first rules they choose together.

**Intent cue:** Tell the difficult truth

### Chapter 3 — The Border Progress

Investigate an attack designed to restart a war.

**Scene beat:** Liora and Ren tour the frontier under the guise of a ceremonial courtship, questioning soldiers who would never speak freely to an enemy envoy. A standard stitched from forged loyalties unfolds into The False-Flag Shrike and scatters evidence toward both kingdoms. The pair follow what it tries hardest to destroy, finding orders written in Spymaster Orr's private cipher.

### Chapter 4 — Flowers in Burned Fields

Let shared grief restore contested ground.

**Scene beat:** Two villages claim the same surviving well, each with a memorial proving the other kingdom burned its fields. Liora and Ren stop arguing over whose history is complete and publicly name what both armies destroyed. The unburied grief charges from the ash as The Cinderhart, carrying a different list of dead on every branch of its antlers. When both villages speak every name, flowers follow its path and reluctant trust takes root around a shared channel.

**Intent cue:** Name what was lost

**Boss / escalation:** The Cinderhart

### Chapter 5 — The Masque of Two Courts

Separate forged affection from a freely chosen truth.

**Scene beat:** At a diplomatic masque, forged messages implicate both royal families while every dance is treated as evidence of the envoys' staged romance. Liora and Ren set boundaries the treaty cannot choose for them, then admit that the attraction left outside those boundaries is real. The masque's prescribed steps assemble into The Clockwork Chaperone, forcing every gesture toward a politically useful ending. Breaking its rhythm reveals the courier who planted Orr's letters and lets Liora and Ren leave the dance on their own terms.

**Intent cue:** Unmask the forgery

**Boss / escalation:** The Clockwork Chaperone

Face the truths both crowns buried before peace.

### Chapter 7 — Orr's Last Proof

Expose a conspiracy built to look inevitable.

**Scene beat:** Orr arranges an assassination attempt whose evidence points cleanly to both kingdoms, certain that frightened courts will accept contradiction as proof of treachery. Liora and Ren retrace the conspiracy together, matching forged dispatches to the people ordered to carry them. Beneath the council chamber, The Proof-Eater consumes any fact that joins one side's story to the other. The envoys feed it Orr's isolated lies in the right sequence, forcing the missing connections back into view before both courts.

**Intent cue:** Reveal Orr's design

**Boss / escalation:** The Proof-Eater

### Chapter 8 — A Treaty Without Chains

Replace two bound envoys with a peace everyone can own.

**Scene beat:** With Orr exposed, both crowns demand that Liora and Ren preserve the magical bond as the price of peace. Instead, the envoys gather border towns, veterans, traders, and grieving families to negotiate terms no pair of exceptional people must carry alone. The old treaty closes around them as The Sovereign Knot, a living constellation of crowns and compulsory vows. They dissolve it clause by clause, then freely choose both a public peace and a private future that no magic can enforce.

**Intent cue:** Write a freer peace

**Boss / escalation:** The Sovereign Knot
