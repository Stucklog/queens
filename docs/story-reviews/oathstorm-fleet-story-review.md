## How to use this worksheet

Review in order and write only the decision-relevant note at each checkpoint. Start with the story pass, then the hero and cast, then chapter backgrounds and encounter sprites, and finish with palette and system consistency. Mark anything that needs a change with a clear action verb: rewrite, remove, add, reposition, recolor, rename, or verify.

### Fast review route

- [ ] Story pass — read the arc title, tagline, chapter titles, captions, and scene copy without looking at art.
- [ ] Art pass — inspect the hero, supporting characters, opponents, bosses, finishers, and backgrounds at actual display size.
- [ ] Consistency pass — compare palette, silhouette language, contrast, naming, and emotional escalation across chapters.
- [ ] Action pass — consolidate duplicate notes, rank fixes by impact, and record any final questions for implementation.

### Chapter 1 — Harbor of Glass

Yrsa reads the newborn ice to find a path toward the people stranded beyond it.

**Scene beat:** The funeral vow freezes faster than axes can break it, and every careless step opens a black crack beneath the rescue crews. Yrsa reads the stress lines like timber grain, marking the only route strong enough to bear a ship. The oath raises its first border as the Rimehorn Warden, an antlered mass of harbor ice that charges wherever the clans name an enemy. Yrsa must redirect each fracture without letting fear decide who belongs outside.

**Intent cue:** Chart the glass

### Chapter 2 — The Amendable Keel

An unfinished longship can cross the ice only after Yrsa makes room for a broken promise.

**Scene beat:** Yrsa and Toren arrange the keel's unfinished clauses around the oath Yrsa refuses to name, loosening the ship from its frozen cradle. Young Kell Rime boards carrying several clans' competing promises of protection and none of their trust. The rejected clauses twist ashwood and frozen roots into the Clausewood Jotun, which grips the keel whenever Yrsa tries to hide her past. She cannot destroy it; she must carve an honest opening through what remains unfinished.

### Chapter 3 — Captains at Cross-Purposes

Rival crews need a charter strong enough to hold without making them one clan.

**Scene beat:** Captains join Yrsa for rescue, vengeance, trade, and glory, then nearly wreck the expedition arguing over who commands it. Kell finds the one shared duty that can quiet the flock without crowning a leader.

### Chapter 7 — Heart of the Oathstorm

Every captain must surrender an absolute promise before the fleet can reach the storm's heart.

**Scene beat:** Inside the frozen tempest, each captain confronts the vow that brought them aboard and concedes the harm hidden inside it. Yrsa fits their revisions into one mutual promise, but the keel still waits for the truth she withheld. The king's funeral voice emerges as the Dead King's Word, a spectral command sustained by generations afraid that amendment means betrayal. Yrsa admits whom her own broken oath once hurt, and her confession gives the fleet permission to change.

**Intent cue:** Answer the dead king

### Chapter 8 — Aurora Roads

The clans speak a mutual vow while Yrsa carves its changing paths into the sea.

**Scene beat:** Captains, exiles, and islanders speak the new vow across the fleet: they will defend one another's passage and revisit the promise whenever safety becomes possession. Yrsa carves each concession into the keel, thawing roads that shift with need and consent. The last absolute clauses coil across the sky as the Aurora Worldcoil, a cosmic serpent tightening every road toward permanent winter. The fleet carries the new words from ship to ship until the coil opens into paths of living light.
