## How to use this worksheet

Review in order and write only the decision-relevant note at each checkpoint. Start with the story pass, then the hero and cast, then chapter backgrounds and encounter sprites, and finish with palette and system consistency. Mark anything that needs a change with a clear action verb: rewrite, remove, add, reposition, recolor, rename, or verify.

### Fast review route

- [ ] Story pass — read the arc title, tagline, chapter titles, captions, and scene copy without looking at art.
- [ ] Art pass — inspect the hero, supporting characters, opponents, bosses, finishers, and backgrounds at actual display size.
- [ ] Consistency pass — compare palette, silhouette language, contrast, naming, and emotional escalation across chapters.
- [ ] Action pass — consolidate duplicate notes, rank fixes by impact, and record any final questions for implementation.

### Chapter 1 — A Card from a Shadow

Open the passage that Alis died to reveal.

**Scene beat:** Alis's Shadow leads Oriel through closed reading rooms while the catalog card rearranges shelf marks into a route below the university. Oriel must restore the card's broken sequence before the night porter discovers them. At the final index, a many-tined sentinel called The Index Hart charges along every false branch of the catalog. Oriel corners it with the one call number the university erased, and the hidden stair opens beneath its hooves.

### Chapter 2 — The Price of Entry

Borrow one truth and surrender what it weighs.

**Scene beat:** The murder ledger is sealed by the library's balance, so Oriel offers the memory of her mother's farewell at the station. The page opens to reveal that Alis was killed after tracing stolen research to a celebrated university chair. The surrendered memory takes root as The Mnemonic Briar, tangling true images with comforting inventions. Oriel cuts a path by recording what she can still prove, but leaves unable to recall why her mother looked proud.

### Chapter 3 — The Rival Borrower

Trust the rival who already paid too much.

**Scene beat:** Matias Quill recognizes the hollow edge around Oriel's lost memory and admits that years of his own life are missing from repeated loans. Matias remembers its pattern from a year he cannot remember living, and Oriel follows his notes to bind its wings without demanding another loan.

### Chapter 5 — What We Forgot Together

Believe the evidence after the feeling is gone.

**Scene beat:** A locked testimony demands a memory neither investigator owns alone, and Oriel and Matias sacrifice the night their rivalry became friendship. They emerge with proof that Alis named her killer, yet with every affectionate reason to distrust one another restored to suspicion. The lost night ignites into The Cinder Thesis, which burns their shared notes whenever either acts alone. By following the habits their former selves left in the margins, Oriel and Matias quench it together and choose trust as a present action rather than a recovered feeling.

**Intent cue:** Choose trust again

**Boss / escalation:** The Cinder Thesis

### Chapter 6 — The Prestige of Silence

Expose the institution that turns theft into honor.

**Scene beat:** Alis's murder file opens onto a century of dissent erased, discoveries reassigned, and patrons protected by ceremonial acclaim. Curator Vey argues that the bargains prevented unrest, while Oriel sees how carefully the cost was forced upon people with the least protection. The archive's authorship machine becomes The Citation Engine, stamping stolen names beneath portraits of university heroes. Oriel and Matias reverse its proof chain until every prestigious claim points back to the silenced scholar who made it.

**Intent cue:** Trace every stolen name

### Chapter 7 — Witnesses Beyond the Stacks

Move the testimony where no single curator can erase it.

**Scene beat:** Curator Vey seals every exit and offers Oriel one final private audience, insisting that uncontrolled truth will punish innocent people along with the guilty. Instead, the investigators divide Alis's testimony among printers, street teachers, former borrowers, and the living shadows of other victims. The Tenure Wraith hunts each messenger by wearing the faces of authorities they once trusted. Alis's Shadow teaches the witnesses to compare what they remember, and their overlapping accounts leave the specter no solitary mind to haunt.

**Intent cue:** Send the witnesses outward

**Boss / escalation:** The Tenure Wraith

### Chapter 8 — The Open Catalog

Make the forbidden collection impossible to own.

**Scene beat:** At the master catalog, Vey offers to reveal Alis's final minute in exchange for Oriel's reason for seeking justice. Oriel refuses the bargain and links the catalog to thousands of readers above, accepting uncertainty so the evidence can survive outside any chosen keeper. The library gathers its doctrine of perfect control into The Quiet Constellation, a sky of cold index lights that blanks every unapproved connection. Oriel lets readers draw their own lines between the facts, breaking the pattern and opening every shelf at once.

**Intent cue:** Open the catalog

**Boss / escalation:** The Quiet Constellation

- [ ] Palette — colors are intentional, distinguishable, accessible, and consistent with the story’s emotional arc.
- [ ] Backgrounds — focal areas remain clear behind characters and copy; no important detail is lost to crop or contrast.

- [ ] Naming — titles, captions, IDs, filenames, and visible labels agree.
- [ ] Escalation — chapter art and boss spectacle build toward the finale rather than peaking too early.
- [ ] Polish — no accidental artifacts, broken alpha, awkward tangents, duplicated motifs, or unfinished frames.
