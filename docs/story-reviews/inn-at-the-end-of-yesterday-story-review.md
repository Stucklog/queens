### Chapter 1 — The House at the Crossroads

Learn why memory may be revisited but never rewritten.

**Scene beat:** On Tamsin's first night as innkeeper, she guides a weary guest through a memory of the farewell they regret. When the guest reaches for a different ending, Tamsin steadies the room and helps them hear the love inside the goodbye they actually shared. The house rules march from their book as The Rulebook Porter, enforcing every sentence without mercy or context. Tamsin restores the room by upholding the past while making space for the guest to choose what comes next.

**Intent cue:** Keep the first memory

**Boss / escalation:** The Rulebook Porter

### Chapter 2 — The Guest Without Yesterday

Open a room built around a day that is gone.

**Scene beat:** A traveler checks in with no memory of the previous day, no gap in their journal, and mud from a road no map remembers. An impossible room appears upstairs, perfectly clean except for a handless clock and one place set for breakfast. The missing day drifts through the room as The Blank Bellhop, politely removing every clue just after Tamsin notices it. She anchors the traveler's remaining memories long enough to hear bells from three nearby villages.

**Intent cue:** Enter the empty room

**Boss / escalation:** The Blank Bellhop

### Chapter 3 — Hours on the Menu

Recover the tastes and sounds stolen with time.

**Scene beat:** Corin cooks from the traveler's last intact sensations: rain on mint, woodsmoke, and pears just beginning to brown. Each restored flavor points to an hour missing from a different village, where people remember events but not the lives lived inside them. Stolen minutes crawl through the pantry as The Hour-Thieving Ivy, ripening fruit and fading voices with every new leaf. Tamsin and Corin prune its hours in the order they were taken, preserving a sensory trail back to the inn.

**Intent cue:** Cook the lost hours

**Boss / escalation:** The Hour-Thieving Ivy

### Chapter 4 — The Twelve-Year Letter

Follow a delivery route that redraws itself each night.

**Scene beat:** Pip admits that his undelivered letter returns to his satchel every dawn with a different address. Its newest route marks match the stolen village hours and end at a door Tamsin insists has never belonged to the inn. The folded map bursts into The Routefold Raven, carrying each road toward a safer but false destination. Pip chooses the uncertain route himself, guiding the household to the sealed door and finally ending twelve years of walking in circles.

**Intent cue:** Follow the changing route

**Boss / escalation:** The Routefold Raven

### Chapter 5 — The Auditor at the Door

Prove the refuge is not consuming its guests' futures.

**Scene beat:** Auditor Vale arrives to close the unstable rooms and seal the inn outside time. Their rulings seem heartless until Tamsin sees futures thinning around the villages; if the fractures spread, children may lose tomorrows they have not lived yet. Those endangered possibilities gather as The Future-Horn Stag, branching into a thousand lives that cannot all occupy the same hour. Tamsin works beside Vale to protect the viable paths, earning one night to find the source before the inn is erased.

**Intent cue:** Protect the futures

**Boss / escalation:** The Future-Horn Stag

### Chapter 6 — The Room Tamsin Locked

Remember the wish that first called the inn home.

**Scene beat:** Behind the sealed door, Tamsin recovers the choice she buried: as a frightened child, she begged the crossroads for one day she could change, and that impossible wish helped call the inn into being. She later inherited its key but never withdrew the wish at the heart of its rules. Her suppressed regret opens beneath the floor as The Unremembering Well, which stole the traveler's unclaimed yesterday and village hours while trying to build her a changeable past. Tamsin speaks the memory without editing it, stopping the well from taking anything more.

**Intent cue:** Remember the locked day

**Boss / escalation:** The Unremembering Well

### Chapter 7 — Yesterday Restored

Rebuild one day without smoothing away its grief.

**Scene beat:** The household gathers witnesses from each village and lays their recovered hours around the traveler. Corin restores sensation, Pip restores the route, Vale preserves cause and consequence, and Tamsin leaves every painful moment intact. Competing versions of the day become The Midnight Mosaic, offering each person the past they most wanted instead of the one they shared. By choosing the honest pieces together, they return the traveler's yesterday and every village hour to its rightful life.

**Intent cue:** Restore the missing day

**Boss / escalation:** The Midnight Mosaic

**Is the beat clear, emotionally earned, and distinct from the chapter before it? Note exact copy or structural changes.**

### Chapter 8 — New House Rules

Keep a refuge for understanding the past, not escaping it.

**Scene beat:** With chronology stable, Vale still must close any house founded on a wish to rewrite time. Tamsin gathers former guests to draft new rules: memories may be witnessed with consent, no hour may be taken, and every visitor must return to their own tomorrow. The inn's oldest desire flares in the hearth as The Last-Regret Fire, offering Tamsin the childhood day she once demanded. She lets the vision pass unaltered, and Vale records the wandering inn as a refuge that can finally be allowed to remain.

**Intent cue:** Write the new rules

**Boss / escalation:** The Last-Regret Fire
