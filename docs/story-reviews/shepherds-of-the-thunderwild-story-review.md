### Chapter 1 — The Storm That Never Came

Gather a herd that followed rain into nothing.

**Scene beat:** Asha follows static prints through the dust and arranges resonant shepherd bells to call scattered families away from the dead cloud bank. Each recovered beast adds its pulse to a growing map beneath her feet. The largest straggler, Forkhorn Staticback, is trapped inside a loop of its own frightened lightning and charges every approaching rider. Asha mirrors its heartbeat through the bells until the antlered giant recognizes the herd's answering rhythm.

**Intent cue:** Bring the straggler home

**Boss / escalation:** Forkhorn Staticback

Rebuild a route where compass, cloud, and echo fail.

### Chapter 6 — The City of Borrowed Lightning

Free the matriarch without darkening a million homes.

**Scene beat:** The lost route ends beneath Director Sen's metropolis, where a captive matriarch powers pumps, clinics, cooling halls, and whole neighborhoods. Asha, Boro, and Sen reroute essential loads through stored storm cells, gaining one night to design a freedom the city can survive.

### Chapter 7 — A Grid That Can Migrate

Build a power system that follows instead of cages.

**Scene beat:** Asha proposes seasonal relay towers that gather passing charge and return migration corridors to the herd. Citizens, shepherds, and skeptical engineers must align a moving grid before the city's reserves fail and panic closes the gates. Every failed circuit leaves behind The Brownout Choir, spectral echoes that repeat the city's worst memories of cold and darkness until no one will risk another change. Boro turns the echoes into a shared pulse, and the citizens complete the relay by following vibration when the lights go out.

**Intent cue:** Set the grid in motion

### Chapter 8 — The Great Shared Migration

Carry herd, city, and weather into one living cycle.

**Scene beat:** The matriarch walks free as mobile relays, citizen caravans, and the reunited herd cross the final range together. At the lost calving ground, Asha must place every tower and thunderbeast within the ancient pattern before the continent's divided weather collapses. The gathering fronts become The Skywheel Colossus, a continent-wide storm whose turning bands can nourish every watershed or tear the migration apart. Asha trusts the herd to lead the final alignment, and human hands move the grid around their natural rhythm.
